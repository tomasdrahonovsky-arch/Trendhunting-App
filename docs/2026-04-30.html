EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Today's cultural landscape reveals a fundamental tension between authenticity and performance, between heritage and innovation, and between individual wellness and collective experience. The drinks industry is navigating this through strategic repositioning: legacy players are acquiring trend-spotting capabilities (Terold/Off-Piste), while heritage brands seek cultural legitimacy through experiential activations (Bijou at Sound Bites) and governmental recognition (Uruguay's wine-as-living-culture declaration).

The non-alcoholic and functional beverage space continues its maturation. Henkell Freixenet's flat overall sales mask genuine growth in no-alcohol sparkling wines, while Damm's Daura 0.0% launch explicitly targets the underserved intersection of gluten-free and alcohol-free—a niche-within-a-niche strategy that signals market sophistication. Meanwhile, Modelo's simultaneous launch of both high-ABV (8%) and non-alcoholic variants reveals a bifurcated consumer: the same demographic seeking "bolder, higher-proof experiences" is also driving moderation trends.

A critical insight emerges from the Modelo positioning: Gen Z and Millennials are nostalgic for pre-digital social rituals—"getting ready for a night out" moments from the early 2010s. This represents a profound shift: the generation raised on smartphones craves the analogue anticipation their older siblings experienced. For beverage brands, this opens territory around ritual, preparation, and shared pre-experience moments rather than just consumption occasions.

The health-taxation conversation in Europe (calls for higher alcohol and junk food taxes to cover liver disease costs) signals regulatory headwinds that will accelerate premiumisation in alcohol while creating tailwinds for sophisticated non-alcoholic alternatives. Brands that can deliver genuine complexity, cultural credibility, and social currency without alcohol are positioned for structural advantage.
